Point of View

The agentic enterprise.

Enterprise AI isn’t a stack you buy. It’s an architecture you operate.

The industry has converged on a settled reference design: four functional layers — a System of Context (the data foundation), a System of Work (business logic and workflows), a System of Agency (the AI agents and their orchestration), and a System of Engagement (where humans and agents meet). A Trust spine — governance, compliance, audit — runs across all four. Beneath them sits the model substrate: foundation models that are, increasingly, interchangeable.

I don’t claim to have invented this — the major platforms publish a version of it. What I do is operate it as a vendor-neutral operating model, because the decisive fact about the 2026 architecture is that every layer now carries an open seam by design. Any data lake. Any app. Any agent, via MCP and A2A. Any workspace. The protocols carry interoperability, so the layers no longer depend on a single vendor’s stack.

That reframes the work. The layers are durable; the vendors inside them are swappable. The job isn’t choosing a platform — it’s designing the operating model and the governance spine, then letting the protocols do the connecting. When a vendor draws those open seams into its own reference architecture, it concedes the point: the moat is the architecture, not the SKU.

The layer closest to the human accumulates leverage, because it controls attention and distribution. In a Slack shop that’s Slack; in a Microsoft shop it’s Teams. The law is vendor-neutral; the front door is the customer’s call.

“Where the models run” is a governance question at the substrate, not a feature higher up. Sovereign and regulated deployments are decided on the floor of the stack — which is where the next wave of enterprise control is being fought.

Twelve years deploying enterprise platforms taught me the unglamorous truth underneath all of it: demos sell, audits ship. The win is never the model. It’s the architecture and the governance that let an organization put it into production.
